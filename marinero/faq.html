What is a GMO?

A genetically modified organism (GMO) is created by inserting genes from one species (typically bacteria or a virus) into the DNA of another. This results in a plant with characteristics that wouldn’t occur naturally, such as producing pesticides or the ability to withstand high doses of chemical herbicides.

Currently, GMO varieties of 8 crops are cultivated in the U.S. at commercial scale. These are:

    •    Corn
    •    Canola
    •    Soy
    •    Cotton
    •    Sugar Beets
    •    Alfalfa
    •    Papaya
    •    Zucchini and Yellow Summer Squash

Which ingredients in the food served at Chipotle were previously genetically modified?

Of the eight common GMO crops grown in the U.S., only two were found as ingredients in Chipotle’s food prior to our move to non-GMO ingredients: corn and soy.

Corn. GMO corn flour, corn starch, and a small number of other corn-based ingredients were used in Chipotle’s corn and flour tortillas. We have worked with our tortilla suppliers to remove these ingredients or replace them with non-GMO versions. The white sweet corn in Chipotle’s Roasted Chili-Corn Salsa has always been a non-GMO variety of corn.

Soy. GMO soybean oil was an ingredient in our flour tortillas and used as a cooking oil in our restaurants. Our restaurants now cook with Sunflower Oil and Rice Bran Oil. Our tortillas are now made with non-GMO Canola oil (while canola is often genetically modified, the canola oil in Chipotle tortillas is non-GMO). The organic soybeans used to make the tofu in our Sofritas have always been non-GMO, like all certified organic ingredients.

Why did Chipotle decide to use non-GMO ingredients in its food?

As part of Chipotle’s commitment to serving Food With Integrity, we believe that the food we serve should be made with ingredients raised with care for animals, farmers, and the environment. We’re doubtful that the GMO ingredients that used to be in our food meet these criteria. Here are the three key reasons.

  1. Scientists are still studying the long term implications of GMOs. While some studies have shown GMOs to be safe, most of this research was funded by companies that sell GMO seeds and did not evaluate long-term effects. More independent studies are needed.

    The chemical industry claims that GMOs are widely considered to be safe, but the scientific community has not yet reached a consensus on the long-term implications of widespread GMO cultivation and consumption. For example, in October 2013 a group of about 300 scientists from around the world signed a statement rejecting the claim that there is a scientific consensus on the safety of GMOs for human consumption. These scientists would like to see more research conducted, and they’d like more of the research to be conducted by independent third parties, not the companies marketing GMO crops themselves. Until such studies are conducted, we believe it is prudent to take a cautious approach toward GMOs.
     
  2. The cultivation of GMOs can damage the environment. Evidence suggests that GMOs engineered to produce pesticides or withstand powerful chemical herbicides damage beneficial insect populations and create herbicide resistant super-weeds.

    The companies that sell GMO seeds claim that genetically modified crops are better for the environment because they reduce the amount of pesticides and herbicides used in farming. But this has not been shown to be the case. One recent study by researchers at Washington State University estimated that between 1996 and 2011, pesticide and herbicide use increased by more than 400 million pounds as a result of GMO cultivation. This and other evidence suggests that GMO crops are fueling an escalating chemical arms race with weeds and insects.
     
  3. Chipotle should be a place where people who choose to avoid GMOs can still enjoy eating. In our quest to serve the best ingredients, we decided to replace the few GMO ingredients we were using in our food with non-GMO alternatives so that our customers who choose to avoid GMOs can enjoy eating at Chipotle.

For the last 21 years, Chipotle has been making high-quality whole ingredients prepared using classic cooking techniques accessible to all. To that end, we want our customers to have access to ingredients that have not been genetically modified.

Do the meat and dairy products at Chipotle contain GMOs?

The meat and dairy products we buy come from animals that are not genetically modified. But it is important to note that most animal feed in the U.S. is genetically modified, which means that the meat and dairy served at Chipotle are likely to come from animals given at least some GMO feed. We are working hard on this challenge, and have made substantial progress: for example, the 100% grass-fed beef served in many Chipotle restaurants was not fed GMO grain—or any grain, for that matter. This is an important start, but we recognize that sourcing meat and dairy exclusively from animals never fed GMOs would be a long-term project that would represent further progress in this area.

Is the cheese Chipotle serves made with GMO enzymes?

Most cheese makers in the U.S., including those who supply cheese to Chipotle, use what is called Fermentation-Produced Chymosin (FPC), which is a vegetarian rennet that is classified as non-GMO by the EU and all existing U.S. state GMO labeling laws. While this enzyme is non-GMO, it is produced by bacteria that have been genetically modified. During the production process, the enzyme used to make cheese is completely separated from these organisms and does not contain any GMO material. The bacteria are classified as “processing aids” because they are not used as ingredients to make cheese. For this reason, cheese made with FPC enzyme does not contain any GMO material. All of our cheese suppliers provide us with documentation of the non-GMO status of the enzyme used to make cheese for Chipotle.

Are all of the beverages sold at Chipotle made with non-GMO ingredients?

The fountain sodas we sell are typically sweetened with corn syrup, which is almost always made from GMO corn. We offer a number of other beverages, such as organic milk, organic apple juice, and Izze sodas, which are made without genetically modified ingredients. We also provide packets of artificial sweetener for use in the fountain iced tea we serve. That sweetener, like many artificial sweeteners, contains dextrose, which may also be made from GMO corn.

Does Chipotle’s recent announcement apply to processing aids in addition to food ingredients?

No. While all of the ingredients we use to make the food we serve—such as the corn flour in our corn tortillas, or the organic tofu in our Sofritas—are non-GMO, some of our suppliers may use processing aids that contain GMO material.

The FDA defines “processing aids” as:
  1. Substances that are added to a food during the processing of such food but are removed in some manner from the food before it is packaged in its finished form.
  2. Substances that are added to a food during processing, are converted into constituents normally present in the food, and do not significantly increase the amount of the constituents naturally found in the food.
  3. Substances that are added to a food for their technical or functional effect in the processing but are present in the finished food at insignificant levels and do not have any technical or functional effect in that food.
Processing aids aren’t additives like preservatives, colors, or flavors, which are required to be included on ingredient lists. Instead, they are substances that serve a specific purpose in food production, but either do not appear in the finished food or are present at insignificant levels in the finished food. Fruit and vegetable washes are a good example of a processing aid. We use fruit and vegetable wash to clean the produce we serve in Chipotle restaurants, and some of our produce suppliers use fruit and vegetable washes in the process of packing produce.

Some of the food producers who supply Chipotle may use various processing aids that contain GMO material or are produced by genetically modified organisms.

How does Chipotle know your ingredients are non-GMO?

When we decided in 2013 to disclose the GMO ingredients in our food, we undertook a comprehensive review of all of our ingredients to identify those that were likely to be genetically modified. Most of the ingredients we serve—such as jalapeño peppers, red onions, cilantro, and pinto beans—have not been approved for commercial use in genetically modified form in the U.S. In other words, these ingredients can be safely presumed to be non-GMO. In cases where ingredients in our food do exist in genetically modified form, we have moved to non-GMO alternatives. To get assurance of the non-GMO status of those alternatives, we’ve used ingredients that are organically grown (since intentional use of GMOs is not permitted for organic growers), and/or obtained documentation from the ingredient suppliers attesting to their use of non-GMO ingredients. We also ensure that certain ingredients are tested periodically for the presence of GMOs. In some cases, such as sunflower oil, we use ingredients verified as non-GMO by the Non-GMO Project.

It is important to understand that these protocols may not necessarily result in all of our foods being 100% GMO-free. Due to the potential for cross-contamination in fields, facilities or during shipping, it may be that trace amounts of GMOs are present in our food from time to time. For this reason, we identify our ingredients as “non-GMO” rather than “GMO-free” or other terminology that might be misunderstood as a guarantee of absolutely zero genetically engineered content. The United States currently lacks a regulatory definition for “non-GMO” or similar terminology, but private organizations in the U.S. as well as the European Union have put forth standards that allow for food labeled as non-GMO to contain trace amounts (typically up to 0.9%) of genetically modified material.

Was it expensive to remove GMO ingredients from Chipotle’s food?

Not really. While removing genetically modified ingredients from our food took substantial effort by Chipotle in partnership with our food suppliers, we found the cost increases to be less than we initially expected.

How else has Chipotle taken action on the issue of GMOs?

Beyond being the first national restaurant company to disclose the GMOs in our food and switch to non-GMO alternatives where applicable, Chipotle has voiced its support for GMO labeling initiatives—such as I-522 in Washington and Prop. 37 in California—because we believe all consumers deserve transparency when it comes to food.

Isn’t Chipotle’s decision to switch to non-GMO ingredients for its food mainly about making money?

Chipotle is committed to serving our customers the very best ingredients we can find. At the end of the day, we do not believe GMO ingredients make our food better in any meaningful way. This view, in addition to the three reasons mentioned above, is the main reason we believe that switching to non-GMO ingredients was the right thing to do.

What is Chipotle’s position on GMO labeling?

Transparency has always been an important part of Chipotle’s vision. We believe that the more people know about where their food comes from, the better. For this reason, we support mandatory GMO labeling laws.

Isn’t Chipotle contributing to unnecessary hysteria about GMOs?

When Chipotle first started talking about GMOs, it was in the context of transparency—we were the first large restaurant company to disclose the fact that there were GMOs in our food, and commit to switching to non-GMO alternatives. Now, we want people to understand more about GMOs, whether they are in our food or not. We believe people should be interested in the food they eat and where it comes from, and make choices that make sense for them and their families.

There are real concerns about the health and environmental impacts of GMO foods. People can debate this topic, but those who have looked into the issue know that it is extremely complex. We believe the implications of growing and eating GMOs must be studied more by independent scientists who do not have an incentive to produce particular results. What we do know is that some of the chemicals and toxins used in GMO cultivation have caused health and environmental issues, and that is at the very least a reason to be cautious. Caution and hysteria are not the same thing.

Does Chipotle’s stance on genetically modified foods hurt farmers?

Growing non-GMO ingredients can benefit farmers in a number of ways. Farmers growing non-GMO version of crops that are typically genetically modified—like corn, soy, and canola—typically receive a substantial premium for their non-GMO crops.

While we are committed to sourcing non-GMO ingredients, Chipotle respects the right of every farmer, rancher and grower to follow practices that make sense for their business, even if they do not make sense for ours. Just as we believe that consumers have the right to know what’s in their food and avoid ingredients they choose to, farmers have the right to grow whichever crops they choose.

Where can I go to learn more about genetically modified foods?

There are many resources available online to learn about GMOs, and we encourage everyone to learn more about this issue and anything else that impacts the food they eat. The following links represent a diverse set of views on the topic:

Chiptopia: What is CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS?

Getting rewarded in burritos for eating burritos so you can eat even more burritos is every Chipotle fan’s dream come true. That dream is Chiptopia Summer Rewards—a three-month-long promotion designed to reward you with free food by achieving status levels each month. Chiptopia purchases unlock status levels (MILD, MEDIUM and HOT) and rewards (1 Free Entrée at each level), and those earned rewards can even be used toward your next status level. There is so much awesome here, it’s ridiculous.

Chiptopia: How can I get a CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS CARD?

Chiptopia Summer Rewards cards are available at any participating US Chipotle restaurant. You may also obtain and activate a digital 'card' online at chipotle.com/chiptopia. When you register your physical card, you will automatically get a digital version as well. You cannot, however, get a physical card associated with the same member number if you get a digital card online first. So if you want the tactile pleasure of an actual card, start by picking up a card in one of our restaurants.

Chiptopia: How does it work?

Visit Chipotle as frequently as you'd like per month, using the free entrées you’ve earned as you go. With your 4th, 8th and 11th qualifying purchases on separate days each month (that is, a purchase of $6.00 or more, excluding alcohol or gift cards), you unlock a new status level and receive a reward for one (1) free entrée. These rewards expire 30 days after earned. At the end of each month, your purchase activity and any acquired status level resets back to zero. While monthly activity will reset, any status level reached will be tracked, and will count toward Bonus Rewards, available for redemption at the end of the promotion. Unlocking the same status level in three consecutive months unlocks Bonus Rewards specific to that level. Achievement unlocked!

Chiptopia: How do I register a CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS card?

All cards picked up in-restaurant or created online - must be registered at chipotle.com/chiptopia in order for you to be able to claim any earned rewards.

Chiptopia: What is a digital rewards card?

You can either pick up a rewards card at Chipotle, or you can create a digital rewards card by signing up online. If you have a physical card, you will also get a digital version of it once you register, but if you start with a digital card, you will be unable to get a physical card that can be associated with your unique member number (so if you want to impress your friends with your Chiptopia card, be sure to pick up the physical card first).

Chiptopia: If I forgot my CHIPTOPIA card, can I still get credit for a visit?

Yes, you can either log into your account at chipotle.com/chiptopia to access the digital version of your card, or the cashier can look up your account using your mobile phone number.

A mobile phone number is needed in order to register a Chiptopia card, either physical or digital. If you're getting an "Already Registered" message, the phone number that you've entered is already associated with an existing Chiptopia account. Only one account can be created per unique telephone number. Please double-check the number entered.

Please contact us at chipotle.com/email-us.

If you received a physical card first, the answer is yes. You can use whichever you like because they are both associated with the same member number. However, if you signed up for a digital card first, instead of picking up and registering a physical card from the restaurant, you will only be able to use the digital version.

Rewards earned in September will expire 30 days later (other than Bonus Rewards, which have later expiration dates), but you'll no longer be able to earn rewards on your Chiptopia card after Sept 30th—although you may want to keep your card as a reminder of the most incredible summer ever.

Chiptopia: Do I need to register my CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS card?

Yes, you need to register the card you picked up at Chipotle in order to redeem any rewards you earn. You can register your card at chipotle.com/chiptopia. No register, no rewards. Make registration a priority. Super important. 

Chiptopia: What's a STATUS LEVEL and how do I achieve it?

You need to achieve a STATUS LEVEL in order to earn most rewards. Status levels are achieved by making purchases of at least $6 before tax (and excluding alcohol and gift cards), but only once per day. For example, you need to purchase at least four entrées on four different days within the the same month to achieve MILD STATUS, which earns you a free entrée. Easy and awesome. Then, by coming more times that same month, you can earn even higher status levels and more free entrées. Still easy, more awesome. Learn about the reward levels by clicking here.

Chiptopia: How do I earn Free Chips & Guac for signing up?

If you joined CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS and registered your card on chipotle.com/chiptopia during the month of July, you will EARN your first reward of Free Chips & Guac with your first qualifying purchase (of $6 or greater, before tax, excluding alcohol and gift cards). Then, when you make a qualifying purchase of $6 or more, you'll GET your free chips & guac—you can't just get them on their own; like "hey, give me some free chips & guac".

Chiptopia: What qualifies as a visit?

A single purchase with a total greater than $6 before tax qualifies as a visit. Alcohol and gift cards are excluded.

Chiptopia: Do multiple visits per day count towards my Chiptopia status?

No. Each day you can only earn credit for a single purchase. 

Chiptopia: Can I get credit for buying more than one entrée each day?

No. Each day, only one entrée purchase counts toward your status level. For example, if you bought two entrées in one day — either by coming more than once, splitting the purchase of two entrées into two transactions, sharing your card with a friend, or whatever — you would only get credit for purchasing one entrée that day.

Chiptopia: When I redeem CHIPTOPIA rewards, do they count as a purchase?

Yes! You can redeem entrée rewards earned in Chiptopia to help you achieve the next level. For example, when you earn a free entrée for achieving MILD STATUS, you can redeem that reward and it will count as a purchase on your way to achieving MEDIUM STATUS (as long as you redeem it in the same month you earned MILD STATUS). But just like any qualifying purchase for Chiptopia, you can only have one per day, even if it's your free reward.

Chiptopia: Will other (non-CHIPTOPIA) offers or coupons from Chipotle count as CHIPTOPIA purchases?

No. Other offers from Chipotle cannot be combined with CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS. For example, if you have an offer for a free burrito that you received in the mail, when you redeem that offer it will NOT count as a purchase toward the next status level.

Chiptopia: Do entrées purchased online or with the Chipotle mobile ordering app count as purchases for CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS?

Yes, but only if you select the “In-Store” payment method; then at the time of pick-up have your card swiped, your digital barcode scanned, or provide you mobile number, as you would with any transaction.

Chiptopia: Do entrées ordered through one of your delivery partners, like Postmates, count as purchases for CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS?

No. Since your payment is indirect with delivery partners and not in-restaurant, we’re unable to swipe/scan your card.

Chiptopia: Does a Burritos by the Box or Catering Order count as an entrée purchase?

Yes. If you happen to be ordering for a large group, you can also earn Chiptopia credit for yourself that day. But even if you’re ordering 20 Burritos by the Box, or Catering for 200, it’s only going to count as a single purchase for that day. 

Chiptopia: Does the status I earn one month carry over to the next month?

Nope. Status resets at the beginning of each month. You must achieve your status again each of the three months during CHIPTOPIA SUMMER REWARDS if you are looking to score a Bonus Reward at the end of the promotion. For example, if you achieve MEDIUM status in July, you’ll need to start over in August and and first earn MILD and then earn MEDIUM, just like you did in July. Then do the same in September. 

Chiptopia: What if I come more than the 11 times it takes to achieve HOT STATUS in one month, do my extra visits roll over into the next month?

That would be amazing, but no. Each month, your purchases reset to zero for that month. For example, if you have 15 visits in July, even though only 11 were needed to achieve HOT STATUS, the four extra visits will not count toward August. But it would still be amazing.

Chiptopia: How do I redeem a reward?

Chiptopia rewards can only be redeemed in-restaurant. To redeem your reward, order the item you've earned and present your Chiptopia card to the cashier to swipe or scan at the register. Your reward will be automatically redeemed at the register. Easy peasy.

Chiptopia: How long do I have to redeem my rewards?

Rewards earned in July, August, and September expire 30 days after they’re earned. Bonus Rewards expire December 31, 2016 (except the Chipotle Catering for 20 Bonus Reward earned by achieving HOT status in all three months – that expires March 31, 2017).  So don’t dawdle. 

Chiptopia: Can I choose when to redeem my rewards?

Yes, as long as you redeem them prior to their expiration, you can choose when to redeem them. But you can only redeem one entrée reward each day. And remember, you can use your rewards to help you achieve the next status level because when you redeem them, they count as a visit.

Chiptopia: What is a BONUS REWARD?

BONUS REWARDS are extra rewards you can earn if you achieve one of the status levels (MILD, MEDIUM, or HOT) across all three months: July, August AND September. Learn about the BONUS REWARDS you can earn at chipotle.com/chiptopia.

Chiptopia: How do I qualify to earn a BONUS REWARD?

In order to qualify for the Bonus Rewards, you need to achieve at least MILD, MEDIUM or HOT status EACH MONTH in July, August AND September. For example, if you achieve MILD status each month, you will earn the MILD BONUS REWARD which is an additional free entrée. If you earn HOT status each month, you will earn the Catering for 20 reward. That, would be awesome.

Chiptopia: How do I redeem a BONUS REWARD?

If you qualify for Bonus Rewards at the end of Chiptopia, it will appear in the My Rewards module in your account at chipotle.com/chiptopia. If you’ve achieved a MILD Bonus Reward (a FREE entrée), you’ll redeem your reward by presenting your Chiptopia card to be swiped or scanned at the time of checkout. If you’ve achieved a MEDIUM Bonus Reward ($20 credit for Chipotle gear), you’ll enter your Chiptopia member card number as a promo code at checkout on store.chipotle.com. If you’ve achieved a HOT Bonus Reward (Catering for 20: Burritos by the Box, The Two Meat Spread, or The Big Spread), you’ll redeem your reward by presenting your Chiptopia card to be swiped or scanned at the time of checkout when you pick up your catering order. To redeem a catering Bonus Reward and as with all catering orders, we suggest that you order at least 7 days in advance.

Chiptopia: If I earn different status levels each of the three months, which BONUS REWARD do I earn?

If you earn a status level each of the three months but they are different levels, you qualify for the reward equal to the LOWEST status level you achieve in any one month. For example, if in July and August you earn MEDIUM STATUS, but in September you earn MILD STATUS, you will qualify for the MILD BONUS REWARD.

Chiptopia: If I earn HOT STATUS each of the three months, and earn the HOT BONUS REWARD, do I also get the MEDIUM and MILD BONUS REWARDS?

No. You earn only the reward at the BONUS REWARD level you achieved (but nice try).

Chiptopia: If I achieve HOT status, am I able to choose rewards from lesser status levels instead?

No. And why on earth would you want to do that? You earn only the reward at the BONUS REWARD level you achieved. So, have a party.

Chiptopia: What's up with all the messages on my Chiptopia receipt?

We've created a handy visual guide to your Chiptopia receipts at chipotle.com/chiptopia-receipts.

Chiptopia: What do I do if my card isn’t scanned at the time of purchase?

Doh! Sorry we forgot to scan your card. There isn't a way for customers to add their own visits online (because you can just imagine what that would lead to), but our Customer Service team can help. Please click here to share your full name and 14-digit card number, as well as the restaurant address, date, time, and total of the transaction. They can dig-in to our systems and connect the dots in order to credit your account for the visit.

Chiptopia: Why do you need my mobile number?

We only ask for your mobile number so that we can connect you to your account in case you don't have access to your plastic or digital Chiptopia cards. We do not share any personal information with anyone, ever. We sell burritos, not phone lists.

Chiptopia: Can I use a free burrito reward from a previous month for the first status-qualifying burrito of the next month?

YES! You can cleverly roll your reward from one month over to the next to start you on your way to achieving the next month's status.

Chiptopia: Once I’ve hit HOT status, what happens? Do I get more credit? Does my receipt show my additional visits?

Once you've hit HOT status, you get another free burrito, and you're totally awesome. Other than that, you won't get more credit for additional visits, but you'll be completely prepared to crush it the next month and hit HOT status again, on your way to the big bonus reward.

Chiptopia: Where can I find what Bonus Reward I have earned?

You can check to see what Bonus Reward you have earned starting at 9:00am EST on October 1, 2016. You can use the same page where  you’ve been checking your progress for Chiptopia—www.chipotle.com/chiptopia or feel free to visit any US Chipotle restaurant.

Chiptopia: How do I redeem my Mild Bonus Reward?

You can redeem your MILD Bonus Reward of one FREE entree at any Chipotle restaurant (awesome). Please just present your Chiptopia card at the time of payment.  

Chiptopia: How do I redeem my Medium Bonus Reward?

To redeem your MEDIUM Bonus reward, please visit our gear store at Store.chipotle.com to choose the merchandise you’d like, and enter your Chiptopia number as the promo code prior to checkout. Your reward is good for up to $20 in merchandise, shipping, handling and tax (awesomer) If your total purchase exceeds $20, you will need to cover the remaining balance along with any shipping and handling fees. If you have a physical card, your Chiptopia number is located on the back of your card. If you do not have a physical card, login in to your account at www.chipotle.com/chiptopia to look up your Chiptopia account number.

Chiptopia: How do I redeem my Hot Bonus Reward?

You can claim your HOT Bonus Reward of Catering for 20 (Burritos by the Box, The Two Meat Spread, or The Big Spread) by calling 1-800-CHIPOTLE and placing your order. When you go in to pick up your order at the restaurant you selected, just go straight to the register and simply present your Chiptopia card. The cashier will swipe / scan your Chiptopia card and you’ll be walking out with the awesomerest reward of all.

Chiptopia: When will I be able to receive my Bonus Reward?

Chiptopia ends at 11:59:59 PDT on September 30, 2016. Bonus Rewards will be available starting October 1 at 9am EDT.  

Chiptopia: When does my Bonus Reward expire?

The bonus rewards for Mild and Medium expire on December 31, 2016, so don’t dawdle. The bonus reward for Hot expires on March 31, 2017, so you should have time enough to plan your party.

Chiptopia: I’ve received the Medium Bonus Reward, can I order merchandise on the Gear Store that is more than $20?

You sure can! Think of it as Chipotle cash: add as much to your shopping cart as you’d like and we’ll pick up $20 of your tab. If your total purchase (including tax, shipping and handling) exceeds $20, you will need to cover the remaining balance.

Chiptopia: Can I switch my Bonus Reward for another reward?

Unfortunately, not. If you’ve achieved Medium, your reward will be the Medium reward.

Chiptopia: I have a Chiptopia card and used it but haven’t registered it. Can I still register after September 30?

Registration ends on September 30, 2016 at 11:59:59 PDT. You cannot register your card after then, so endeavor to do it before then or you’ll be out of luck.

Chiptopia: I received the Hot Bonus Reward, can I choose between Catering for 20 and Burritos by the Box?

If Catering for 20 is your Bonus Reward, you have the choice of Burritos by the Box, The Two Meat Spread, or The Big Spread. So pick whichever option works best for you, then go ahead and order that. In some areas, we offer limited Catering, so Burritos by the Box is the only option.

Chiptopia: If I've achieved HOT Bonus Reward (FREE Catering for 20), how do I best redeem my delicious, awesomerest reward?

Start by checking your My Rewards section in your account at chipotle.com/chiptopia to confirm the status of your reward. Then just visit www.chipotle.com/catering for Chipotle Catering details, including what options are available for your area. When you're ready to order, call 1-800-Chipotle to place your order at least 24-hours in advance of your event or party—however we suggest that you order at least 7 days in advance when possible. Then, when you come in to pick up your catering at the location listed on your catering order confirmation, just present your Chiptopia card to be swiped or scanned at the time of checkout.  It's that easy, and so very delicious.

CHIPTOPIA: How do I redeem my bonus rewards if I've lost or inadvertently thrown away my physical Chiptopia Card?

Don't despair. Just log in to your Chiptopia account to access your digital card. See? That was easy. If you have trouble logging in or have any issues with your bonus reward, contact Chipotle customer service by going to chipotle.com/email-us.
